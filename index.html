Spontaneous symmetry breaking is a miraculous phenomenon in physics. The spontaneous breaking of gauge symmetries (an invariance of measurable quantities under a continuous transformation), in particular, is critical to the development of quantum field theory and particle physics. The Higgs mechanism, which is the specific application of spontaneous symmetry breaking of gauge field theories (quantum field theory with gauge symmetry), specifically a breaking of local gauge invariance, is what leads to massive gauge bosons in the weak interaction (the W and Z bosons).
